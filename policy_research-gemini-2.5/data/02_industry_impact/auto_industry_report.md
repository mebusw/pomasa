# Automotive Industry Impact Report
**Date:** January 10, 2026
**Analyst:** Automotive Industry Analyst (02.auto_industry_analyst)

## 1. Industry Overview under New Macro Environment
The automotive industry in 2026 acts as the primary vanguard for the "New Productive Forces" national strategy. The market is characterized by a **"U-shaped" growth trajectory**, where initial sluggishness in Q1 is expected to recover due to strong policy support and the release of new models.
*   **Market Structure:** A decisive shift towards oligopoly is accelerating. The market share of New Energy Vehicles (NEVs) is projected to exceed **60%**, cementing their dominance over internal combustion engine vehicles.
*   **Hyper-Competition:** The industry faces "involutionary" (race to the bottom) competition, with price wars continuing as brands fight for survival and market consolidation.

## 2. Policy Impact on NEVs (New Energy Vehicles)
*   **Tax Policy Adjustment (2026):**
    *   The era of full purchase tax exemption has ended. For 2026-2027, NEVs are subject to a **halved purchase tax (levied at 5%)**, with a maximum exemption cap of RMB 15,000 per vehicle.
    *   **Impact:** This will likely cool down the ultra-low-end market slightly but favor high-value, intelligent models where the tax burden is relatively smaller compared to the vehicle's tech value.
*   **Consumption Stimulus:** Continued trade-in subsidies and "rural promotion" (cars to countryside) policies are deployed to counterbalance the tax hike.

## 3. Policy Impact on Intelligent & Connected Vehicles (ICV)
*   **L3 Autonomous Driving Legalization:**
    *   **Milestone:** Following the first batch of L3 road test licenses issued in late 2025 (to Changan, BAIC/Arcfox, BYD, etc.), 2026 marks the **"Year of Commercial Pilot"**.
    *   **Regulation:** The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) has established a clear liability and access framework. Vehicles can now legally operate in "eyes off" mode in designated zones (Beijing, Chongqing, Shanghai).
    *   **Technology Trend:** "City NOA" (Navigation on Autopilot) is now a standard feature for mid-to-high-end vehicles. The focus shifts from "availability" to "usability" and "safety".

## 4. Supply Chain & Export Implications
*   **Supply Chain Sovereignty:**
    *   Aligned with the "15th Five-Year Plan", automakers are under pressure to increase the localization rate of automotive chips and AI compute modules.
    *   **Battery Prices:** Expected to rise slightly due to raw material cycles, putting pressure on OEM margins.
*   **Export Strategy:**
    *   Despite geopolitical friction (tariffs from EU/US), exports are forecast to grow by **>10%**.
    *   **Strategy Shift:** From "exporting vehicles" to "exporting production". Chinese OEMs are accelerating the construction of overseas factories (in SE Asia, Hungary, Mexico) to bypass trade barriers.

## 5. Risk Assessment
*   **Profitability Crunch:** The combination of price wars, reduced tax incentives, and rising battery costs will severely squeeze profit margins.
*   **Geopolitical Risk:** Potential for new trade barriers targeting "smart car" data security and software.
*   **Overcapacity:** The rapid expansion of production capacity for "New Productive Forces" may lead to localized oversupply, forcing consolidation of smaller players.
